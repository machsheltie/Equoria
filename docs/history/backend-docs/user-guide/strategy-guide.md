### Understanding the Competition System

#### **Horse Level Calculation**
Your horse's competitive level is calculated from:
- **Base Stats** (40% weight) - Core attributes like speed, stamina, agility
- **Training Scores** (30% weight) - Discipline-specific skill development
- **Trait Bonuses** (20% weight) - Epigenetic trait advantages
- **Age Factor** (10% weight) - Peak performance ages 6-8

#### **Level Scaling System**
- **Levels 1-10**: Every 50 points (0-499 total points)
- **Levels 11-20**: Every 100 points (500-1499 total points)
- **Level 21+**: Every 150 points (1500+ total points)

### Discipline-Specific Strategies

#### **Racing Disciplines (Speed-Focused)**
**Primary Stats**: Speed (40%), Stamina (35%), Agility (25%)
- **Breeding Focus**: Select for high speed and stamina genetics
- **Training Priority**: Racing discipline training weekly
- **Trait Targets**: ATHLETIC, COMPETITIVE, FAST
- **Age Strategy**: Compete heavily ages 3-8, retire by 12
- **Competition Path**: Start with shorter races, build to longer distances

#### **Dressage (Precision-Focused)**
**Primary Stats**: Precision (40%), Intelligence (30%), Obedience (30%)
- **Breeding Focus**: Calm temperament, high intelligence lines
- **Training Priority**: Dressage training with patience
- **Trait Targets**: INTELLIGENT, GRACEFUL, FOCUSED
- **Age Strategy**: Peak performance ages 6-12, longer career possible
- **Competition Path**: Master basic movements before advanced patterns

#### **Show Jumping (Athletic-Focused)**
**Primary Stats**: Agility (35%), Balance (35%), Boldness (30%)
- **Breeding Focus**: Athletic build, confident temperament
- **Training Priority**: Show jumping with courage building
- **Trait Targets**: BRAVE, ATHLETIC, COORDINATED
- **Age Strategy**: Compete ages 4-14, watch for soundness
- **Competition Path**: Start low, gradually increase jump heights

#### **Western Disciplines (Versatility-Focused)**
**Primary Stats**: Balance (30%), Obedience (30%), Intelligence (25%), Agility (15%)
- **Breeding Focus**: Calm, trainable horses with cow sense
- **Training Priority**: Western training with cattle exposure
- **Trait Targets**: CALM, INTELLIGENT, COW_SMART
- **Age Strategy**: Long careers possible, ages 3-16
- **Competition Path**: Master one discipline before diversifying

## üß¨ Advanced Breeding Strategies

### Genetic Optimization

#### **Trait Stacking Strategy**
1. **Identify Target Traits** - Choose 3-4 key traits for your discipline
2. **Parent Selection** - Both parents should carry desired traits
3. **Generational Planning** - Build traits over 3-4 generations
4. **Outcrossing** - Introduce new bloodlines to avoid inbreeding
5. **Record Keeping** - Track trait inheritance patterns

#### **Statistical Breeding**
- **Stat Averaging** - Foals inherit average of parent stats ¬±10%
- **Dominant Traits** - Some traits have higher inheritance rates
- **Hybrid Vigor** - Outcrossed foals often exceed parent averages
- **Regression Risk** - Inbreeding can reduce overall quality

### Bloodline Development

#### **Foundation Breeding**
1. **Acquire Foundation Stock** - Invest in high-quality base horses
2. **Establish Lines** - Develop 2-3 distinct bloodlines
3. **Line Breeding** - Concentrate desired traits within lines
4. **Outcrossing** - Cross lines to combine best traits
5. **Selection Pressure** - Only breed the best performers

#### **Specialized Programs**
- **Racing Program**: Focus on speed and stamina genetics
- **Sport Horse Program**: Emphasize athleticism and trainability
- **Pleasure Program**: Prioritize temperament and movement
- **Multi-Discipline Program**: Develop versatile, athletic horses
