## üê¥ Horse Management

### **Q: Why can't I train my horse?**
**A:** Horses must be at least 3 years old to train. Also check:
- Training cooldown (7 days between any training)
- Horse health (must be Good or better)
- For Gaited training, horse must have the Gaited trait

### **Q: How often can I train my horse?**
**A:** Each horse can train once per week (7-day cooldown) in ANY discipline. This means one training session total per week, not one per discipline.

### **Q: What's the difference between horse stats and discipline scores?**
**A:** 
- **Stats** (0-100): Natural abilities like Speed, Stamina, Agility
- **Discipline Scores**: Learned skills in specific competitions like Racing or Dressage
- Both affect competition performance, but discipline scores can be improved through training

### **Q: When do horses retire?**
**A:** Horses automatically retire from competition at age 21, but can continue breeding until natural death.

### **Q: How do I improve my horse's health?**
**A:** 
- Provide regular groom care
- Avoid overworking (respect training cooldowns)
- Ensure proper rest between competitions
- Some health issues heal naturally over time

## üèÜ Competition System

### **Q: Why did my best horse lose to a weaker horse?**
**A:** Competition includes realistic variance factors:
- Age performance curves (peak at 6-8 years)
- Trait bonuses and penalties
- Random performance variation
- Discipline-specific stat weighting
- Training level in that discipline

### **Q: How are competition results calculated?**
**A:** The system uses:
- Base stats (40% weight)
- Training scores (30% weight) 
- Trait effects (20% weight)
- Age factors (10% weight)
- Plus realistic performance variance

### **Q: What disciplines can my horse compete in?**
**A:** Equoria features 24 disciplines! Most horses can compete in any discipline, but:
- Gaited competitions require the Gaited trait
- Some disciplines favor certain stats (Speed for Racing, Precision for Dressage)
- Training in a discipline improves performance in that area

### **Q: How do I enter competitions?**
**A:** 
1. Ensure your horse meets requirements (age 3-21, good health)
2. Find an appropriate show for your horse's level
3. Pay the entry fee
4. Wait for competition results

### **Q: What do I win from competitions?**
**A:** 
- **Prize Money**: 1st place gets 50%, 2nd gets 30%, 3rd gets 20%
- **User XP**: 20/15/10 XP for 1st/2nd/3rd place
- **Horse XP**: 30/27/25 XP for 1st/2nd/3rd place
- **Potential Stat Gains**: Random stat increases for top 3 finishers

## üß¨ Breeding & Genetics

### **Q: How does breeding work?**
**A:** 
1. Both mare and stallion must be 3+ years old
2. Foals inherit average stats from parents (¬±10% variation)
3. Traits can be passed from either parent
4. Gestation period varies by breed
5. Foal care in first 6 days is critical for development

### **Q: What are epigenetic traits?**
**A:** Special characteristics that develop based on care, environment, and experiences rather than just genetics. Examples include BRAVE, INTELLIGENT, ATHLETIC. They can be positive, negative, or neutral.

### **Q: How do I get better traits in my foals?**
**A:** 
- Provide excellent care during the first 6 days
- Use professional grooms for foal enrichment
- Ensure both parents have good traits
- Create optimal environmental conditions
- Complete milestone activities during critical periods

### **Q: Can I breed horses that are related?**
**A:** The game prevents close inbreeding, but distant relatives can be bred. However, outcrossing (unrelated horses) often produces better results.

### **Q: When can my foal start training and competing?**
**A:** 
- **Training**: Age 3 years
- **Competition**: Age 3 years  
- **Breeding**: Age 3 years
- **Foal Enrichment**: Ages 0-2 years only

## üë• Groom Management

### **Q: Do I need grooms?**
**A:** While not required, grooms significantly improve horse development and care. They're especially important for:
- Foal development (ages 0-2)
- Competition preparation
- Daily horse care and bonding
- Trait development

### **Q: What's the difference between groom personalities?**
**A:** 
- **Calm**: Best for nervous or high-strung horses
- **Energetic**: Great for lazy or unmotivated horses  
- **Methodical**: Ideal for systematic training programs

### **Q: Why did my groom retire?**
**A:** Grooms have a 2-year (104 week) career limit. Level 10+ grooms can also choose early retirement. Plan ahead by hiring replacements!

### **Q: What is the groom legacy system?**
**A:** Retired level 7+ grooms can mentor new hires (prot√©g√©s) who inherit some of their mentor's abilities and start with experience bonuses.

### **Q: How do groom talents work?**
**A:** Grooms unlock talent trees at levels 3, 5, and 8. Each personality type has different available talents that provide permanent bonuses to their services.

## üí∞ Economy & Progression

### **Q: How do I make money in Equoria?**
**A:** 
- **Competition winnings** (main source)
- **Breeding fees** from successful horses
- **Horse sales** to other players
- **Stud services** from champion horses

### **Q: What should I spend money on?**
**A:** Priority order:
1. **Groom services** (best return on investment)
2. **Quality breeding stock** (long-term investment)
3. **Training costs** (improve performance)
4. **Competition entries** (earn more money)
5. **Facility upgrades** (when available)

### **Q: How does the XP system work?**
**A:** 
- **User XP**: Gained from competitions, training, breeding activities
- **Horse XP**: Separate system where horses earn XP from competitions
- **Horse XP Conversion**: 100 Horse XP = 1 stat point you can allocate
- **Leveling**: User levels unlock new features and capabilities

### **Q: What happens when I level up?**
**A:** Higher user levels unlock:
- Access to better grooms
- Advanced breeding options
- Higher-tier competitions
- Additional stable capacity
- Special features and tools
