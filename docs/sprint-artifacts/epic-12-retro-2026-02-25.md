## What Went Well

### 1. Parchment Theme Consistency (10/10)

**Achievement:** All 3 new horse profile tabs (Pedigree, Health/Vet, Stud/Sale) correctly use the parchment palette. No Celestial Night elements leaked in.

**How:** MEMORY.md entry created during the first horse profile tab work: "Uses parchment/fantasy theme. All new tabs must use: bg-parchment, text-aged-bronze, text-midnight-ink, border-aged-bronze, text-burnished-gold."

**Result:** Visually cohesive horse detail page.

### 2. Sticky Action Bar Design (9/10)

**Feature:** The action bar hovers at the bottom of the screen with a backdrop blur — players can always see their primary horse actions without scrolling.

**Benefit:** No "where do I train this horse?" confusion. The 5 most common actions are always one tap/click away.

**Implementation detail:** `pb-20` padding prevents the last tab content from being hidden behind the bar — a detail that's easy to miss without testing.

### 3. Gender-Aware Stud/Sale Tab (8/10)

**Feature:** The Stud/Sale tab shows "List for Stud" option only for stallions, "Sale Listing" for all horses.

**Benefit:** Contextually appropriate — mares and geldings can't be listed for stud. No irrelevant UI elements.

### 4. Weekly Reward UX (8/10)

**Pattern:** Claim button shows "Claimed ✓" state after click. Visual feedback is immediate; the reward is concrete (+500 coins). Players understand the weekly cadence instantly.

### 5. TabType Union Extension (9/10)

**Pattern:** Extending the existing `TabType` union (`'overview' | 'stats' | 'training' | 'traits'`) with new values (`'pedigree' | 'health' | 'stud-sale'`) was clean. TypeScript enforced correct tab values everywhere.

## What Didn't Go Well

### 1. Two Stable Pages Potential Confusion (5/10)

**Problem:** There are now two separate pages:

- `/stable` — the horse list/grid (StableView.tsx, existing)
- `/my-stable` — the stable profile/Hall of Fame (MyStablePage.tsx, new)

These are conceptually related but visually and functionally different. New players may not understand the distinction.

**Fix:** Info panels on both pages explain their purpose. Breadcrumbs indicate different sections.

**Recommendation:** Add cross-links ("View your stable profile →") between the two pages.

### 2. Inventory Empty State Too Common (6/10)

**Problem:** The initial game state has no items in inventory. Players always see the empty state. This makes the Inventory page feel useless until they acquire items.

**Fix:** The mock data (when `USE_MOCK_DATA=true`) shows sample items. But with live API, it's genuinely empty for new players.

**Recommendation:** New player onboarding should award a starter kit (1 training saddle, 1 bridle) to populate the inventory immediately.

### 3. Action Bar Disabled States (5/10)

**Problem:** Feed and Assign Rider are disabled but show no clear timeline for when they'll be enabled. "Coming soon" tooltip is vague.

**Recommendation:** Change to "Available in [feature name]" tooltip with more specificity (e.g., "Feed your horse at the Feed Shop").

## Critical Decisions

### Decision 1: Parchment Theme for HorseDetailPage Only

**Approach:** Standalone pages (Bank, Inventory, My Stable) use Celestial Night. HorseDetailPage uses Parchment/Fantasy.

**Result:** Horse detail page has a distinctive "document/record" feel that makes it feel special — like examining an official stud book entry.

**Verdict:** ✅ Correct. Dual themes are intentional and clearly documented.

### Decision 2: `/my-stable` Separate From `/stable`

**Approach:** Two distinct routes: `/stable` = horse grid, `/my-stable` = stable profile.

**Rationale:** "My Stable" as a management view (stats, branding, Hall of Fame) is conceptually different from "My Horses" (the working stable list).

**Verdict:** ✅ Correct conceptually. Needs better wayfinding to help players find both.

### Decision 3: Action Bar as Fixed Bottom Layer

**Approach:** `position: fixed`, `bottom: 0`, `z-index: 50`, backdrop blur.

**Result:** Always visible, never interferes with tab content (protected by `pb-20`).

**Verdict:** ✅ Correct. Mobile-first pattern that works equally well on desktop.

## Known Issues Discovered

### Action Bar Height Not Responsive

The action bar is fixed-height. On very small phones (iPhone SE), 5 buttons may wrap or compress. Needs responsive testing on narrow viewports.

### Hall of Fame Empty in Live Data

The Hall of Fame tab shows retired horses. No horses have been retired yet in the production database (game is new). Empty state shows but won't self-populate until horses retire.
